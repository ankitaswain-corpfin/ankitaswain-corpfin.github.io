Home

If there’s one constant in my life, it’s that I am always in the middle of a story, sometimes reading it, sometimes writing it and often accidentally living it.
By day, I work with numbers and narratives of a very practical kind like cash flows, forecasts, the quiet drama of decisions on a balance sheet. By night and most weekends, I trade spreadsheets for stories. I host Baby Blue Book Club, a small experiment that started with two friends and has grown into a cozy, opinionated corner of the internet where thirty-plus readers and a few hundred lurkers argue lovingly about books.
When I’m not talking about stories, I’m usually consuming them in sitcoms, crime thrillers, horror movies, or the YA fantasy novel I’m slowly, stubbornly writing. I cook Indo‑Chinese food for comfort, unroll my yoga mat for sanity, and blog about money, fashion, and literature because I like my worlds a little bit mixed, logic with aesthetics, discipline with daydreaming and intentions with actions.
